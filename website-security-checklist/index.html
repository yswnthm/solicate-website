Website Security

The 2026 Website Security Checklist for Indian Businesses

A hacked website doesn't just look bad—it can get you blacklisted by Google, destroying years of SEO work overnight.

The Checklist

  • SSL Certificate (HTTPS): Non-negotiable. Google Chrome warns users if your site is not secure.
  • WAF (Web Application Firewall): Blocks malicious bots and DDoS attacks before they hit your server.
  • Daily Automated Backups: Kept off-site. If you get hacked, you need to restore the site in minutes.
  • Strict Plugin Auditing: Remove inactive plugins and keep active ones updated weekly.
  • Strong Passwords & 2FA: For all CMS and hosting logins.

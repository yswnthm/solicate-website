Why Is My Website Not Performing?

"My website is not performing" covers three different problems that look the same from the outside: no traffic from Google, traffic with no enquiries, or enquiries that do not convert to clients. Each has a different root cause and a different fix.

What "performing" actually means for a business website

A website is performing when it does three things reliably:

1. It gets found — appearing on Google for the searches your buyers use in your city. This is a traffic and SEO problem.
2. It builds trust — convincing a buyer who has never heard of you that you are the right choice. This is a proof and content problem.
3. It generates enquiries — getting that buyer to message, call, or submit a form. This is a conversion and UX problem.

Most underperforming websites have at least two of these three broken. The most common combination: not getting found (SEO problem) and not building trust (content/proof problem).

Diagnosing your specific performance gap

If you get zero organic traffic from Google

The problem is SEO structure. Your page titles are generic, your service pages are thin or non-existent, you have no local signals, and Google cannot tell what city you serve or what you specifically do.

Fix priority: Rewrite page titles with city + service keywords. Create one page per service. Add LocalBusiness schema. Set up or fix your Google Business Profile. Submit sitemap to Google Search Console.

If you get traffic but no enquiries

The problem is trust and conversion. Buyers are finding you but not believing in you or not being able to reach you easily.

Check these in order:

  • Is there a visible WhatsApp button on mobile?
  • Is there a portfolio or proof of recent work?
  • Does the page describe what you do specifically — or is it generic?
  • Is there any pricing signal or starting-from range?
  • Does the site load in under 3 seconds on a mobile connection?

If you get enquiries but they are low quality

The problem is targeting and qualification. Your page is attracting the wrong buyer — someone who cannot afford your service, needs something you do not offer, or is not in your service area.

Fix priority: Be more specific on your service pages about who the service is for, the investment range, and what the process looks like. Specificity filters out the wrong leads before they enquire.

The technical performance layer

Beyond SEO and conversion, there is a technical layer of performance that Google measures directly:

Largest Contentful Paint (LCP) — how long it takes for the main content to appear on screen. Should be under 2.5 seconds.
Interaction to Next Paint (INP) — how responsive the page is when a user taps or clicks. Should be under 200ms.
Cumulative Layout Shift (CLS) — how much the page layout jumps as elements load. Should be under 0.1. Layout shifts on mobile are a common problem with portfolio-heavy websites.

Check your scores at pagespeed.web.dev. A mobile score below 50 is a ranking penalty in competitive local searches.

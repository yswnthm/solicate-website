Why Your Business Website is Not Getting Enquiries (And How to Fix It)

Published: July 12, 2026 | Reading Time: ~19 mins | Author: Solicate Team

A website not getting enquiries usually suffers from one of two core problems: it either lacks organic traffic entirely (the visibility problem), or it has visitors but fails to convert them into leads (the conversion pathway problem).

When your business inbox is quiet, it is easy to assume the design is outdated. However, the root causes are usually structural and technical. In this guide, we will analyze the seven main conversion killers and show you how to turn your website into a lead generation tool.

Diagnosing the Problem: Traffic vs. Conversion

Before making changes, you need to identify where your pipeline is broken.

The Traffic Gap

Look at your Google Search Console. If your site gets fewer than 100 organic impressions a month, you have a traffic problem. Even a high-converting layout cannot generate leads without visitors. Your primary focus should be on technical SEO, local keywords, and content depth.

The Conversion Gap

If you have traffic from social media, newsletters, or search terms, but no form submissions or calls, you have a conversion pathway problem. The average B2B conversion rate ranges from 2% to 5%. If your conversion rate is below 1%, the structure of your site is likely hurting leads.

The 7 Conversion Killers on Business Websites

1. Buried Call-to-Actions (CTAs)

If a visitor has to scroll to the bottom of the page or search through menus to find your contact details, many will leave. Your primary call-to-action should be visible above the fold on all device sizes. High-contrast CTA buttons in the header help keep the enquiry path clear.

2. Missing WhatsApp Integration on Mobile

Many mobile users prefer direct messaging over calling or filling out forms. If your website lacks a visible WhatsApp contact option on mobile, you are likely missing out on potential leads. A tap-to-chat button helps reduce friction for warm enquiries.

3. Form Field Fatigue

Long contact forms with numerous required fields can turn away visitors. Keep your forms as simple as possible. Ask only for the essential details needed to initiate the conversation: Name, Email/Phone, and a brief message.

4. Lack of Case Studies and Testimonials

Service businesses build trust through proof. If your site lacks case studies, client logos, or testimonials, visitors may look elsewhere for verified work. Display actual project timelines and outcomes rather than generic descriptions.

5. Confusing Service Pages

If a visitor cannot understand what you offer and how you deliver it within 30 seconds, they will leave. Structure your service pages clearly. Use bulleted lists to break down features, define deliverables, and provide starting price guidelines.

6. Page Load Latency

Slow page speeds directly impact conversion rates. A 1-second delay in page load time can reduce conversions by up to 7%. Bloated templates and unoptimized images are the main causes of mobile load delays.

7. No Pricing Signals

Without any pricing indications, visitors may assume your services are out of their budget. Including a starting price or a target range helps qualify leads and builds transparency early in the process.

What a Perfect Enquiry Path Looks Like

To maximize contact rates, structure your website's layout like an intentional sales pipeline:

  1. Land on a specific service page: Ensure clients searching for a specific niche land directly on that page rather than your generic homepage.
  2. Highlight proof above the fold: Display visual outcomes, reviews, or client logos instantly.
  3. Prominent call-to-action (CTA): Display a single, clear button (e.g. "Request a free 15-minute video CRO review").

FAQs

What is a good conversion rate B2B service websites?

A standard conversion rate is 2% to 5%. If your site converts less than 1% of visitors, the structure needs immediate attention.

Why is my contact form not getting submissions?

Buried form placement, lengthy fields, and lack of visual case study links are the primary culprits.

How do I make my website more trustworthy?

Include clear testimonials, named client showcases, secure SSL certificates, and direct contact details in your footer.

How does mobile speed affect conversion rates?

A 1-second delay in page load time can reduce conversions by up to 7%. Fast pages reduce bounce rates and help keep users engaged in the enquiry process.

Should B2B sites display starting prices?

Yes. Displaying a starting price or target range filters out unqualified traffic and sets clear expectations with potential customers before they reach out.

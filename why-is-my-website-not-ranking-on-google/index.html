Why Is My Website Not Ranking on Google?

If you search Google for your service in your city and your website does not appear, you are losing leads every single day to businesses that have figured out the structural signals Google looks for. Here is why your website is not ranking — and what each fix looks like.

1. Your page titles do not match what buyers search

Google reads your page title first. If your homepage title is just "Home" or your business name — like "Riya Events" — Google has no idea what service you offer or what city you serve.

A title like "Wedding Planner in Kakinada, Andhra Pradesh — Riya Events" tells Google: this page is about a wedding planner, in Kakinada, in AP. That single change can move you from invisible to page 2 within weeks.

Fix: Rewrite every page title as: [Service] in [City, State] — [Business Name]. Do the same for your H1 heading on each page.

2. You have one generic "Services" page instead of individual pages

If you offer three services — say, wedding planning, event decoration, and mehendi — and they all live on one "Services" page, Google can rank that one page for at most one of those searches. Often none.

Every service you offer needs its own dedicated page with its own title, H1, and content. That is how Google decides what each page is specifically about.

Fix: Create one page per service. Each page should target one clear search: "wedding planning Kakinada", "event decoration Rajahmundry", "mehendi artist East Godavari".

3. Your website has no local SEO signals

Google uses a combination of signals to decide whether your business is relevant for searches in a specific city:

  • LocalBusiness schema — a structured block of code that tells Google your name, address, phone, and service area
  • Google Business Profile — your listing on Google Maps and the local pack (the 3-business block at the top of local searches)
  • City mentions in content — your city name appearing naturally in headings, body copy, and metadata
  • NAP consistency — your Name, Address, and Phone number matching exactly across your website, GBP, and any directories

Most small business websites in India have none of these. Adding just LocalBusiness schema and a properly configured GBP can move you into the local pack for your city within 4–8 weeks.

4. Your website is slow on mobile

Google uses mobile-first indexing — it ranks your site based on how it performs on a phone, not a desktop. If your website takes more than 3 seconds to load on a mobile connection, Google penalises your ranking and buyers leave before they even read your content.

Common causes of slow mobile loading: uncompressed images, too many Google Fonts loaded, heavy JavaScript libraries, no lazy loading on gallery images.

Check your speed: Go to pagespeed.web.dev and enter your website URL. A mobile score below 70 is a ranking problem.

5. You have no sitemap or robots.txt

Google needs to crawl your website to index it. A sitemap tells Google which pages exist and how important they are. Without one, Google may miss your service or location pages entirely — especially if they are not linked prominently from your homepage.

A robots.txt file tells Google what it is allowed to crawl. A missing or misconfigured robots.txt can accidentally block Google from indexing your entire site.

6. Your page has no content Google can read

If your services page is mostly images, a slideshow, or text embedded inside an image, Google cannot read it. Google reads text — HTML text. A page with 50 words of real text will often outrank a visually impressive page with no readable content.

Every service page needs at least 300–500 words of real, specific content explaining what you do, who it is for, where you serve, and what buyers should expect.

7. Your domain is too new

If your website has been live for less than 3–6 months, Google may still be deciding how trustworthy it is. This is normal. The fix is not to wait — it is to consistently publish indexed pages, get your GBP live, and earn a few links from local directories or press mentions. That compounds trust faster than time alone.

The hidden cost of a cheap template

Templates look good on the demo page, but they come with a massive hidden tax: Code Bloat.

To allow anyone to change colors and layouts without coding, template builders load megabytes of JavaScript and CSS on every page. This ruins your Core Web Vitals score. Google penalizes slow sites, meaning your template website will struggle to ever reach Page 1.

When to use a Template

  • You have zero budget right now.
  • It is just a hobby project or temporary holding page.
  • You do not rely on Google for clients.
  • You are driving 100% of your traffic through paid ads (and even then, low conversion rates will hurt you).

When to go Custom

  • You want organic, free leads from Google search.
  • You operate in a competitive local market (like real estate or healthcare).
  • Your services are high-ticket (trust is paramount).
  • You want a permanent sales asset that works 24/7.

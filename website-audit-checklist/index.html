Website Audit Checklist: The 30-Point Small Business SEO & CRO Guide

Published: July 6, 2026 | Reading Time: ~15 mins | Author: Solicate Team

Many business owners know their website isn't bringing in leads, but they don't know why. Before hiring an agency or spending money on redesigns, you should conduct a structured audit of your digital asset. This guide provides a comprehensive 30-point website audit checklist tailored for small businesses in India. It covers key parameters in local SEO, technical performance, and conversion design, helping you identify exactly what is holding your website back.

Section 1: The Local SEO Checklist (10 Points)

If local clients can't find you on Google, your website is missing out on high-intent local traffic. Audit these 10 points first:

  • [ ] Do you have a verified Google Business Profile with matching name, address, and phone details?
  • [ ] Is your physical address marked up using LocalBusiness schema code in your website head?
  • [ ] Are your latitude and longitude coordinates embedded in your site's geo schema?
  • [ ] Have you built dedicated landing pages for every city or region you actively serve?
  • [ ] Does your page title tag include your target service and city (e.g., "Wedding Planner in Vijayawada")?
  • [ ] Is there an embedded Google Map on your contact page matching your verified listing?
  • [ ] Have you added regional testimonials from real clients on your city-specific pages?
  • [ ] Do you have clean BreadcrumbList schemas configured to help search crawlers map your site?
  • [ ] Are local phone numbers and contact details clearly visible in your site footer?
  • [ ] Is your site registered in reputable local Indian business directories?

Section 2: Technical Performance & Speed (10 Points)

Site speed is directly tied to search engine rankings and visitor retention. Run your site through Google PageSpeed Insights and check these 10 items:

  • [ ] Does the page load in under 2 seconds on a standard mobile 4G connection?
  • [ ] Have you set up style preloads in your head tag to improve Largest Contentful Paint (LCP)?
  • [ ] Are all site images compressed and served in modern formats (WebP/AVIF)?
  • [ ] Have you set up lazy loading for images located below the fold to save bandwidth?
  • [ ] Is your page HTML markup validated and free of major coding syntax errors?
  • [ ] Is there an active SSL certificate (HTTPS) configured for secure browsing?
  • [ ] Does your robots.txt file prevent search engines from crawling low-value administrative paths?
  • [ ] Have you submitted a structured sitemap.xml index to Google Search Console?
  • [ ] Are canonical tags set up on every page to prevent duplicate content penalties?
  • [ ] Is the website fully responsive and free of horizontal scrolling bugs on mobile screens?

Section 3: Conversion Path & Trust (10 Points)

A fast, high-ranking website is useless if it doesn't guide visitors toward making an enquiry. Evaluate your conversion path against these 10 points:

  • [ ] Is there a clear primary CTA button (e.g. "Get a Free Audit") visible above the fold?
  • [ ] Does a floating WhatsApp button allow mobile users to start a chat instantly?
  • [ ] Are contact forms short, requiring no more than 4 essential fields?
  • [ ] Have you placed real client testimonials close to your primary CTA buttons?
  • [ ] Do your testimonials display verified client details, names, and project context?
  • [ ] Is your core value proposition clearly stated in the main hero heading?
  • [ ] Have you set up Google Analytics (GA4) conversion tracking for your form submissions?
  • [ ] Do you show clear value signals or starting pricing guides to filter leads?
  • [ ] Are there clickable phone number links ('tel:') and email links ('mailto:') in your footer?
  • [ ] Is the copy free of generic jargon, focusing on the specific problems you solve for clients?

How to Leverage Your Audit Findings

Once you check off these 30 items, compile a list of your uncompleted tasks. Prioritize fixing your technical speed and local SEO schemas first—these represent the foundation of your online visibility. Next, optimize your CTA copy and layout flow to start capturing more enquiries from your existing website traffic.

Deep-Dive Technical Considerations & Field Notes

In our experience revamping websites across various Indian cities, we consistently find that basic optimizations are neglected. For example, image asset management is often misunderstood. Many creators think that setting width and height attributes in CSS is sufficient. In reality, modern browsers require explicit aspect-ratio declarations and source-set mappings to prevent layout shifts. Layout shifts (measured by Cumulative Layout Shift or CLS) directly affect search rankings and user satisfaction.

Furthermore, web font rendering is a silent killer of Largest Contentful Paint (LCP). When a visitor lands on a website, the browser has to fetch the external font files (such as Google Fonts Inter or Outfit). While the font is downloading, the browser either hides the text (Flash of Invisible Text - FOIT) or shows a system font (Flash of Unstyled Text - FOUT). Both states degrade user experience. We solve this by implementing the font-display swap parameter and preloading critical web fonts, ensuring text renders instantly.

# Product Guidelines: Solicate Website

This document outlines the core content and design rules for the Solicate website, focusing on text-first authority.

---

## 1. Core Philosophy: Content Over Design
- **Priority**: Content is the primary driver of value. We focus on writing rich, authoritative, and deep text content (topical clusters, detailed locations, industry guides, and case studies).
- **Design Role**: The design serves purely to make the content highly readable, digestible, and accessible. We avoid complex design gimmicks, decorative clutter, or distracting layouts.

---

## 2. Visual Design (Paper & Ink Theme)
- **Backgrounds**: Warm, orange-tinted paper background (`--paper`: `#e9ddce`, `--paper-dark`: `#dbd2c4`) to facilitate prolonged reading.
- **Text & Ink**: High-contrast dark ink (`--ink`: `#111111`) for maximum text legibility. Mid-tone ink for secondary text (`--ink-mid`: `#444444`).
- **Accent**: Subtle orange (`--accent`: `#f97316`) used sparingly for interactive highlights.
- **Typography**: Clean, readable sans-serif (*Inter*) and monospace (*IBM Plex Mono*) fonts.
- **Layout**: Clean, structured grids with border-width `1.5px` and container width limited to `1200px`.

---

## 3. Prose Style & Copywriting
- **Tone**: Direct, authoritative, and results-driven.
- **Form**: Short, punchy sentences. High use of bullet points and numbered lists to structure long-form content.
- **Substance**: Practical insights, raw data, case study proof, and clear explanations. No marketing fluff or filler text.

---

## 4. Technical & Performance Standards
- **Page Load Speed**: Perfect performance (LCP < 2.5s) to guarantee instantaneous reading access.
- **Markup**: Clean, semantic HTML5 structure.
- **Scripts**: Lightweight vanilla JS used only where interactive tools (e.g. ROI Calculator) add direct value.

---

## 5. SEO & Accessibility
- **Semantic Structure**: Proper heading hierarchies to organize long-form content clearly for readers and search crawlers.
- **Local & Service Schema**: JSON-LD schema (FAQ, LocalBusiness, Service) embedded in HTML to help search engines parse our content hubs.

Why is my website not getting enquiries?

When a website gets visitors but no enquiries, the issue is usually not traffic. It is trust, clarity, or friction.

A visitor should never have to hunt for the next step after seeing proof they like.
LeakHow It Shows UpFix
Weak proof flowGallery impresses, then the page goes cold.Add service context and CTA beside proof.
Generic CTAOnly a buried "Contact us" page.Use WhatsApp, call, booking, or audit-specific CTAs.
Unclear offerVisitors cannot compare services or packages.Explain categories, inclusions, and next steps.

Your proof is not connected to action

A portfolio gallery can impress visitors and still fail commercially. After someone sees your work, the page should answer what the service includes, where you work, who it is for, what the next step is, and how to contact you. If the contact path is hidden, the lead dies.

Your services are too vague

Buyers do not enquire when they cannot understand what you sell. Wedding planners need planning, decor, venue coordination, guest logistics, and packages. Photographers need pre-wedding, wedding, candid, traditional, albums, and delivery timelines. Each service needs concrete explanation.

Your CTA is buried or too generic

"Contact us" is weaker than "Send your wedding date on WhatsApp" or "Request an SEO revamp audit." Good CTAs match the buyer's next step. They should appear after proof, after pricing signals, after FAQs, and near the top of mobile pages.

Better CTA pattern: action + context. Example: "Send your wedding date on WhatsApp" beats "Submit."

Your site may be slow on mobile

Visual businesses often upload heavy images directly from shoots. Slow galleries reduce trust and increase abandonment. Compress images, set dimensions, lazy load non-critical images, and keep the main enquiry path fast.

What to fix first

  • Add proof near each service, not only on one gallery page.
  • Use direct WhatsApp, call, booking, or form CTAs.
  • Explain service categories and package differences.
  • Add FAQs that handle buyer hesitation.
  • Track CTA clicks and form submissions.

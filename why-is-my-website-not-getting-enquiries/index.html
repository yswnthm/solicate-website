Why Is My Website Not Getting Enquiries?

A website not getting enquiries usually has one of two problems: it is not getting traffic at all, or it is getting traffic but failing to convert visitors into leads. Each has a different fix.

Problem 1: No traffic — the SEO problem

If your website gets fewer than 50 visits per month from Google, the issue is not your enquiry path — it is that no one is finding your site in the first place. A perfectly designed contact form generates zero leads from zero visitors.

The fix is structural SEO: page titles with city and service keywords, dedicated service pages, local schema, a live Google Business Profile, and mobile speed above 70 on PageSpeed Insights.

Read: Why is my website not ranking on Google? — a full breakdown of the structural reasons.

Problem 2: Traffic but no enquiries — the conversion problem

If you are getting traffic (from Google, Instagram, referrals, or WhatsApp shares) but no one is submitting a form, calling, or messaging you, the problem is the enquiry path — the route a buyer takes from landing on your page to making contact.

Here are the most common conversion killers:

No WhatsApp button

In India, most buyers want to message before they call. If your website does not have a visible WhatsApp link — especially on mobile, where the tap-to-chat is instant — you are losing the majority of your warm leads. A floating WhatsApp button visible on every page is one of the highest-ROI changes you can make to a business website in AP and Telangana.

The contact form is buried

If a buyer has to scroll to the very bottom of your homepage, click "Contact Us", fill in 7 fields, and submit — most will not. The contact path should be visible within 2 seconds of landing on any service page. For visual businesses, a "Request a free consultation" or "Get a quote on WhatsApp" button directly below the hero works best.

No proof — gallery, testimonials, or past work

For wedding planners, photographers, event studios, and makeup artists, trust is visual. A buyer comparing three photographers will choose the one whose website shows the most relevant, high-quality work — with a clear timeline, pricing range, and review or two from past clients. If your website has no portfolio, no testimonials, and no named clients, buyers leave to find someone who does.

Unclear service pages — "What exactly do you do?"

If a buyer lands on your services page and cannot answer "What will I get, how long does it take, and roughly what does it cost?" in 30 seconds — they leave. Not because they are not interested. Because the cognitive effort is too high. Every service page needs a clear description, a what's included list, and a starting price or a free audit offer.

No pricing signal at all

You do not need to publish exact packages. But if your website gives no indication of price range — not even "Starting from ₹25,000" or "Investment ranges from ₹X to ₹Y" — buyers assume either you are too expensive or too cheap for their budget. A price range filters out the wrong leads and qualifies the right ones before they even contact you.

The full enquiry path — what it should look like

01

Buyer finds you on Google or Instagram

This requires: correct page title, local SEO signals, and a live GBP for Google. For Instagram, a link in bio to a dedicated landing page.

02

They land on a relevant service page

Not your homepage. The page that matches exactly what they searched — "wedding planner Kakinada" should land on your wedding planning page, not a generic home.

03

They see your proof immediately

Gallery, testimonials, or a named project. Within 5 seconds of landing. Not after scrolling through a long intro paragraph.

04

They tap the WhatsApp button or call CTA

Visible, prominent, and above the fold on mobile. "Chat on WhatsApp" or "Get a quote" — one clear action, not five options.

How to diagnose your own enquiry path

Open your website on your phone. Pretend you are a buyer who has never heard of your business. Time how long it takes you to find how to contact you. If it takes more than 10 seconds, you have a conversion problem. If you rank for nothing on Google, you have a traffic problem first.

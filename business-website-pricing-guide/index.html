Business Website Pricing Guide: What You Should Actually Pay For

A business website should not be priced like a set of pages. It should be priced by the business job it needs to perform: explain the offer, build trust, get discovered, and generate qualified enquiries.

For most small and growing Indian businesses, a useful website package starts around ₹40,000. A stronger SEO-first business website usually falls between ₹75,000 and ₹2,50,000 depending on structure, copy, content depth, and functionality.

What a proper business website package should include

  • Page strategy: homepage, service pages, about, proof, FAQs, contact, and relevant local pages.
  • Copy and messaging: clear offer, audience, problem, outcomes, process, objections, and next step.
  • Custom design: mobile-first layouts, brand fit, visual hierarchy, trust sections, and conversion flow.
  • Development: fast, responsive, crawlable pages that do not depend on heavy visual builders.
  • SEO basics: titles, descriptions, headings, schema, canonical tags, internal links, and sitemap coverage.
  • Conversion setup: WhatsApp, call buttons, forms, lead magnets, audit CTAs, and analytics events.
  • Launch support: testing, redirects where needed, form checks, mobile checks, and handoff.

What usually increases pricing

The quote rises when the website needs more than a basic build. Common scope additions include:

  • Detailed service pages for each offer instead of one generic services page
  • Location pages for local SEO in multiple cities or regions
  • Industry pages for vertical positioning and BOFU searches
  • Original copywriting, case study writing, and FAQ development
  • CMS setup for team editing, blogs, projects, products, or careers
  • Ecommerce, booking, filters, dashboards, forms, payment, or third-party integrations
  • Technical SEO cleanup, speed optimization, redirects, and migration planning

How to compare website quotes correctly

Do not compare only the final number. Compare what the quote actually includes.

Weak quote

  • Lists page count but not page purpose
  • No content, SEO, schema, or sitemap details
  • No mention of speed or mobile testing
  • No enquiry tracking or conversion plan
  • No maintenance or update plan after launch

Strong quote

  • Explains the page map and buyer intent behind each page
  • Includes metadata, schema, internal links, and sitemap coverage
  • Defines copywriting, proof, FAQ, and local content responsibility
  • Includes WhatsApp, form, call, and tracking requirements
  • States what is included after launch and what costs extra
